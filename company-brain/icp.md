# Ideal Customer Profile

## Summary

Bravenger's ideal customer is a Series A/B B2B SaaS company that has product-market fit, is scaling its go-to-market, and is hitting the wall where positioning lives in the founder's head and content contradicts itself across teams.

---

## Firmographics

| Attribute | Criteria |
|-----------|----------|
| **Stage** | Series A or Series B |
| **Funding** | $5M–$50M raised |
| **Headcount** | 20–150 employees |
| **Revenue** | $1M–$15M ARR |
| **Industry** | B2B SaaS (horizontal or vertical) |
| **Founder profile** | Technical founder or AI-native founding team |
| **GTM maturity** | Has initial traction, now building repeatable GTM |
| **Geography** | North America primary, English-speaking markets |

## Technographics

| Attribute | Criteria |
|-----------|----------|
| **Content stack** | Markdown-friendly, or willing to adopt it |
| **AI adoption** | Already using AI tools (Cursor, Claude, ChatGPT, Copilot) for engineering; starting to explore for GTM |
| **Tooling** | GitHub/GitLab, Notion or similar, Slack, HubSpot or Salesforce (early), Linear or Jira |
| **Content creation** | Mix of founder-written, agency, and AI-generated — no system governing any of it |
| **Data maturity** | Product analytics in place (Amplitude, Mixpanel, PostHog); GTM analytics patchy |

## Pains

These are the real problems we solve. They show up in every discovery call.

### P1: Positioning trapped in the founder's head
The founder knows the product cold. They can pitch it perfectly. But that knowledge has never been written down in a way that anyone else can use. Every new hire, every agency, every AI tool starts from scratch.

### P2: First marketer with no foundation
They just hired their first Head of Marketing or first PMM. This person inherited a blog with 12 posts, a pitch deck from the seed round, and a Notion page titled "messaging" that hasn't been updated in 8 months. They're expected to produce a website, launch campaigns, and build a content engine — with no source of truth to work from.

### P3: AI tools with no context
The team is using Claude, ChatGPT, or Jasper to produce content. But every prompt starts from zero. There's no shared context, no persona definitions, no approved messaging. The AI produces plausible-sounding content that contradicts the pitch deck, the website, and the sales talk track — often in the same week.

### P4: SE and product knowledge that doesn't scale
Solutions engineers and product experts carry deep use-case knowledge in their heads. When they leave or get overloaded, that knowledge disappears. New SEs take months to ramp. Customer calls get worse. Competitive positioning gets stale.

### P5: Use cases undocumented
The product supports 8-12 use cases, but nobody has written them down in a structured way. Sales doesn't know which ones to lead with for which persona. Marketing writes about features instead of outcomes. CS can't build onboarding paths because nobody has mapped triggers to outcomes.

### P6: Content that contradicts itself across teams
Marketing says one thing on the website. Sales says something different in the pitch. Product describes the roadmap in a way that doesn't match either. CS tells customers things that aren't true yet. There's no single source of truth, so every team invents its own version of the story.

### P7: Positioning decays after every offsite
The team did a positioning exercise 6 months ago. It produced a slide deck. Nobody reads it. The market moved. The product changed. New people joined who never saw it. The positioning is technically correct and practically useless.

### P8: No way to measure alignment
Even if they wanted to fix drift, they can't measure it. There's no way to compare what the website says versus what sales says versus what the product actually does. Drift is invisible until a customer calls it out.

## Buying Signals

These indicate a company is ready for Bravenger now.

1. **Just hired first Head of Marketing or first PMM.** New GTM hire with no foundation. They need to build it fast.
2. **Post-positioning exercise frustration.** They did the workshop. They have the slides. Nothing changed. They want a system, not a document.
3. **AI content initiative underway.** Team is experimenting with AI for content but getting inconsistent results. They know they need shared context.
4. **Website rewrite or rebrand in progress.** Redesigning the site forces the "what do we actually say" conversation. They need the answer before the design.
5. **SE team growing or turning over.** Adding SEs or losing experienced ones. Knowledge transfer is painful. Use case documentation doesn't exist.
6. **Series A/B just closed.** New capital means new hires, new GTM push, and new urgency to get messaging right before they scale it.
7. **Multi-product or multi-persona complexity.** Company has grown beyond a single product or single buyer. The simple story doesn't work anymore.
8. **Competitive pressure increasing.** Category is getting crowded. Differentiation matters more. They can't afford to be sloppy with positioning.
9. **Founder starting to step back from sales.** Founder can't be on every call anymore. The team needs to pitch without the founder in the room.
10. **Content volume increasing without quality.** Publishing more but converting less. The volume is there but the alignment isn't.

## Buying Process

### Decision-Making Unit

| Role | Function | File |
|------|----------|------|
| Technical Founder (CEO/CTO) | Economic buyer, final sign-off | `personas/persona-founder.md` |
| Head of Marketing | Champion, day-to-day buyer | `personas/persona-head-marketing.md` |
| First PMM | Champion, Brain owner post-delivery | `personas/persona-first-pmm.md` |
| VP/Head of Product | Technical evaluator | `personas/persona-vp-product.md` |

### Typical Process
1. **Trigger.** One of the buying signals above. Usually the new marketing hire or the post-offsite frustration.
2. **Discovery.** 30-minute call. We diagnose the current state: where does positioning live, who produces content, what tools are in use, where is the drift.
3. **Proposal.** Fixed-price proposal ($10-15K). Scope is clear: one Company Brain, built in one week, covering ICP, personas, use cases, positioning, messaging, governance, and agent guidelines.
4. **Evaluation.** Founder and marketing lead review the proposal. Product lead may evaluate the architecture. Decision is fast — usually 1-2 weeks.
5. **Delivery.** One-week engagement. We interview stakeholders, audit existing content, build the Brain, deliver a working repo with agent guidelines.
6. **Adoption.** PMM or Head of Marketing owns the Brain going forward. Agents read from it. Content aligns.

### Budget
- **Range:** $10,000–$15,000 one-time
- **Budget holder:** CEO/founder or marketing budget
- **Comparison:** Less than one month of a freelance content strategist. Less than one agency engagement. Produces a permanent asset, not a deliverable that decays.

## Anti-ICP

Do not pursue these companies. They will waste time and not succeed with the product.

| Signal | Why it's a bad fit |
|--------|-------------------|
| **Pre-PMF** | No stable positioning to codify. The Brain would be obsolete in weeks. |
| **Enterprise (500+ employees)** | Procurement cycles, legal reviews, stakeholder complexity. Not our motion. |
| **Non-technical founders who resist Markdown** | The Brain is a repo. If they want a slide deck, we're not the right fit. |
| **No AI adoption intent** | If they won't use AI agents to consume the Brain, the system doesn't deliver value. |
| **Agency-dependent GTM** | If all content is outsourced and they want to keep it that way, the Brain has no internal consumer. |
| **Single-person companies** | No alignment problem to solve. The founder IS the brain. |
| **Companies that want "just messaging"** | We build a system, not a messaging doc. If they want a one-pager, refer them to a positioning consultant. |
