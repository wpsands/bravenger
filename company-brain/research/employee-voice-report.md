# R-003: AI & the Employee Voice

**Full title:** "AI & the Employee Voice: From Questionnaires to Conversations"
**Authors:** Charlie Woodward (CEO), Dan Willard (CDO), Matthew Noble (Head of AI & Data)
**Date:** Late 2024 / Early 2025
**Methodology:** 240 semi-structured interviews (~50 min each) with People Listening leaders from 34 countries, 6 continents, 200 companies. Then 420 peer-to-peer conversations via Natter across same population in 40 minutes. 29,810 qualitative statements captured, 492,184 words transcribed, 96 themes and insights generated.

## Interview vs. Conversation Differences

These five differences are the product thesis — they explain why Natter exists:

1. **Breadth vs. Depth:** Interviews captured broad themes but lacked nuanced exploration. Conversations went deeper into cultural barriers, psychological safety, and actionable strategies.
2. **Problem vs. Solution focus:** Interviews centered on identifying problems. Conversations emphasized solutions — practical ways forward, multi-channel feedback systems, inclusive designs.
3. **Cultural/Geographic perspectives:** Conversations uniquely highlighted cultural and geographic distinctions. This was absent in interviews.
4. **Employee-centric vs. Organizational-centric:** Conversations explored employee wellbeing, mental health, job satisfaction. Interviews leaned toward organizational/system considerations.
5. **Obligation vs. Motivation:** Interviewees are reactive respondents to an authority. Conversation participants are proactive collaborators motivated to teach and learn from peers.

## Marc Solow (Deloitte) Key Insights (paraphrase only)

- The biggest challenge isn't collecting sentiment — it's the last mile: what do you do with it, how do you close the loop
- Organizations want to move away from traditional annual surveys toward always-on listening
- Survey fatigue isn't about frequency — it's about lack of tangible outcomes from feedback
- There are amazing products like Natter that help conduct focus groups and develop insights at scale
- It's early days for AI in employee listening — most tools are nascent in NLP insight development
- Huge opportunity for technology to gather data at scale and develop insights quickly
