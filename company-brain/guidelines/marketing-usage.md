# Marketing Agent Usage Guide

This guide governs all agents producing marketing content for NovaCRM. Read AGENTS.md first — this file supplements, not replaces, the core governance rules.

---

## Role of Marketing in the Brain

Marketing produces content that attracts, educates, and converts the ICP. Every piece of marketing content must:
- Target a specific persona from `personas/`
- Reference at least one use case from `use-cases/`
- Use approved value propositions from `messaging-positioning/messaging-framework.md`
- Follow the voice and positioning in `messaging-positioning/core-positioning.md`
- Include citations using `[Source: filename#section]` format

## Content Bill of Materials (BOMs)

### Blog Post BOM
Before writing a blog post, assemble:
- [ ] Target persona (which persona file?)
- [ ] Primary use case (which use case file?)
- [ ] Funnel stage (awareness, consideration, decision?)
- [ ] Primary value proposition (which VP from messaging-framework.md?)
- [ ] Customer outcome hook (what does the reader achieve?)
- [ ] CTA (what should the reader do next?)

### Landing Page BOM
- [ ] Target persona (primary + secondary)
- [ ] Hero messaging (aligned to core-positioning.md positioning statement)
- [ ] 3-5 value propositions with approved language
- [ ] Competitive differentiation section (vs. specific named alternative)
- [ ] Proof points (customer outcomes, metrics, case studies)
- [ ] Objection pre-handling (anticipate and address in copy)
- [ ] CTA (demo request, free trial, ROI calculator)

### Email Campaign BOM
- [ ] Target persona
- [ ] Buying signal (what triggered this outreach?)
- [ ] Sequence length (3-5 emails)
- [ ] Value prop per email (don't repeat, build)
- [ ] Personalization hooks (company stage, tech stack, team size, hiring signals)
- [ ] CTA per email (escalate from soft to direct)

### Social Post BOM
- [ ] Platform (LinkedIn primary)
- [ ] Content type (pain-agitation, proof, contrarian take, customer outcome)
- [ ] Primary message (one point per post)
- [ ] Source citation (traceable to Brain files)
- [ ] Engagement hook (question, specific number, bold claim)

### Ad Copy BOM
- [ ] Platform (LinkedIn Ads, Google Ads)
- [ ] Target persona
- [ ] Primary message (one value prop per ad)
- [ ] Competitive angle (what are we replacing?)
- [ ] CTA (direct, benefit-oriented)

## Mandatory Rules for All Marketing Content

### Rule 1: Always Lead with Customer Outcome, Never with AI Features
The customer doesn't buy AI. They buy better forecasts, better coaching, faster onboarding. Lead every piece of content with what the customer achieves. Mention AI only as the mechanism after the outcome is established.

**Do:** "Forecast from behavior, not deal stages."
**Don't:** "Our AI analyzes rep behavior patterns using machine learning."

### Rule 2: Use "AI-Native," Never "AI-Powered"
We are positioning a new category. "AI-powered" sounds like a feature bolted onto an existing CRM. "AI-native" signals a fundamentally different architecture.

**Do:** "NovaCRM is an AI-native CRM."
**Don't:** "NovaCRM is an AI-powered CRM platform."

### Rule 3: Name the Alternative, Not Just the Problem
Every piece of content should make the reader compare NovaCRM against something specific — their current CRM, their current forecasting process, their current coaching approach. Vague problem statements don't drive action.

**Do:** "If your Monday pipeline review is a guessing game, your CRM is the problem."
**Don't:** "Sales teams face many challenges in today's market."

### Rule 4: Mid-Market Specificity Over Generic Claims
Reference team sizes (10-80 reps), company sizes (100-500 employees), and mid-market realities (no dedicated Salesforce admin, tight budgets, fast onboarding requirements). Generic "enterprise" or "business" language signals we don't understand our audience.

### Rule 5: Competitive Positioning by Category, Not Feature
Never do feature-by-feature comparisons. Position by category: "reporting tool vs. selling tool," "AI-powered vs. AI-native," "stripped-down enterprise vs. purpose-built mid-market."

### Rule 6: Every Claim Must Be Grounded
If you claim a customer outcome, reference the use case that delivers it. If you claim a differentiator, reference core-positioning.md. No unsourced claims.

## Channel-Specific Rules

### Website
- Every page targets a specific persona or use case.
- Hero messaging derived from approved positioning statement.
- All product claims cite a use case with accurate status.
- Competitive pages position by category gap, not feature comparison.

### Blog
- Every post targets one persona and one use case.
- Lead with a specific, felt pain — not a generic industry trend.
- Include a proof point or customer outcome in every post.
- End with a CTA that matches the funnel stage.

### LinkedIn
- One point per post. Be opinionated and specific.
- Use the "rearview mirror vs. windshield" framing for CRM category positioning.
- Write from the founder's voice or a customer's perspective — company page voice is secondary.
- Reference specific numbers: team sizes, forecast accuracy, onboarding timelines.

### Email
- Signal-based outreach only. No spray-and-pray.
- Personalize to the buying signal and persona, not just the name.
- Subject lines reference the pain, not the product.
- Soft offers first: "See how we forecast from behavior" before "Book a demo."

### Paid Ads
- One value prop per ad. No feature lists.
- Competitive ads target specific alternative keywords (e.g., "Salesforce alternative for mid-market").
- Landing pages must match ad messaging exactly — no bait-and-switch.

## Drift Detection for Marketing

Marketing content drifts when:

1. **Feature-leading.** Content describes AI capabilities before customer outcomes.
2. **Category confusion.** Content says "AI-powered" instead of "AI-native."
3. **Persona blurring.** Content targets "sales teams" generically instead of a specific persona.
4. **Generic claims.** Content could describe any CRM, not specifically NovaCRM.
5. **Stale comparisons.** Competitive claims don't reflect the current landscape.

### How to check
1. Read the content piece.
2. Identify the target persona. If unclear, flag it.
3. Check that the lead is a customer outcome, not a feature.
4. Verify "AI-native" category language, not "AI-powered."
5. Confirm every claim maps to an approved VP or use case.
6. Check competitive framing against core-positioning.md.
