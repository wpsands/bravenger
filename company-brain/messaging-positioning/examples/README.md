## Conference Messaging

### Talk Title Options
1. "The Company Brain: How We Stopped Fighting About Messaging"
2. "Your AI Tools Need Context, Not Better Prompts"
3. "One Brain, Five Teams, Zero Drift: Building a Source of Truth for Series A/B SaaS"

### Abstract (for submission)

Every Series A/B SaaS company hits the same wall: the founder knows the product cold, but that knowledge doesn't scale. Marketing says one thing. Sales says another. Product describes the roadmap differently. AI tools produce content that sounds good and says nothing accurate.

In this talk, I'll show you our actual Company Brain — the structured knowledge base that every Bravenger agent reads before producing any output. I'll walk through the architecture (ICP, personas, use cases, positioning, messaging, governance), show real examples of aligned vs. drifted content, and demonstrate how a one-week engagement produces a permanent source of truth for the whole company.

This isn't theory. This is the system we use. You'll leave with a clear architecture you can build yourself — or hire us to build.

[Source: README.md#dogfooding-statement] [Source: messaging-positioning/core-positioning.md#positioning-statement]

### Key Slides / Talking Points
1. **The drift problem.** Show real examples of messaging contradictions across teams. (Use anonymized customer examples or Bravenger's own pre-Brain state.)
2. **The architecture.** File map. Foundation layer → Messaging layer → Governance layer. Keep it visual.
3. **Live demo of the Brain.** Open the repo. Show a persona file. Show a use case. Show AGENTS.md. Let the audience see the structure.
4. **Agent in action.** Give an agent a task ("write a blog intro for the Technical Founder persona about UC-001"). Show the cited output. Show the sources.
5. **The dogfooding proof.** "This slide deck was informed by our Brain. This talk track was checked against our positioning. We run on what we sell."
6. **Call to action.** "Browse our Brain on GitHub. If you want one for your company, it takes a week."
