# Messaging Framework

## Value Propositions

### VP1: One Source of Truth
**Statement:** Every team reads from the same Brain. Marketing, Sales, PMM, Product, and CS all produce content from one structured source. No more contradictions.

**Approved language:**
- "One brain. Aligned teams. Zero drift."
- "Every team reads the same source before they say anything."
- "One place to update. Every output corrects itself."

**Forbidden language:**
- "Single pane of glass" (overused enterprise jargon)
- "360-degree view" (meaningless)
- "Centralized hub" (sounds like a wiki, not a system)

**Dogfooding hook:** "This Brain is our source of truth. The marketing page, the sales outbound, and the product brief you just read all came from the same files." [Source: brand-vision.md#core-values]

---

### VP2: Agent-Native Architecture
**Statement:** The Brain is built for AI agents, not just humans. Structured data, YAML frontmatter, governance rules, and citation requirements mean agents produce accurate, aligned output — not plausible-sounding guesswork.

**Approved language:**
- "Your AI tools are only as good as the context you give them."
- "Structured context for agents. Accurate output for teams."
- "Agents read the Brain, cite the sources, produce aligned content."

**Forbidden language:**
- "AI-powered" (we're not an AI product; we're context for AI products)
- "Intelligent" / "smart" (anthropomorphizing the system)
- "Automate your content" (we're not a content automation tool)

**Dogfooding hook:** "Every piece of Bravenger content is generated by agents reading this Brain. The citations are real. The sources are real. The alignment is measurable." [Source: AGENTS.md#citation-requirements]

---

### VP3: Built in One Week
**Statement:** A Company Brain is a one-week engagement. Fixed price. Clear deliverables. You go from scattered positioning to a working system in five business days.

**Approved language:**
- "One week. Fixed price. Working system."
- "You get a foundation that would take a quarter to build yourself."
- "Short enough to be decisive. Thorough enough to be durable."

**Forbidden language:**
- "Quick and easy" (dismissive of the complexity)
- "Turnkey" (sounds like a vendor pitch)
- "Instant" (nothing meaningful happens instantly)

**Dogfooding hook:** "Bravenger's own Brain was built in one focused push. The architecture was proven, the process was repeatable. That's why we can deliver it in a week."

---

### VP4: Use Cases as Foundation
**Statement:** Use cases are not an afterthought. They are foundational — alongside ICP and personas. Every use case has a multi-persona schema, honest status fields, documented gaps, and connections to other use cases. They feed every discipline.

**Approved language:**
- "Use cases feed everything — marketing content, sales discovery, product roadmap, CS onboarding."
- "Each use case tells you who sets it up, who uses it, and who evaluates it."
- "Status reflects reality, not aspiration. If it's partially supported, the Brain says so."

**Forbidden language:**
- "User stories" (different concept, different audience)
- "Workflows" (implies steps; use cases hold strategic knowledge)
- "Customer journeys" (too marketing-specific; use cases are cross-discipline)

**Dogfooding hook:** "Look at UC-001 in our Brain. It's simultaneously our marketing topic, our sales pitch, our product scope, and our CS delivery process. That's what a foundational use case looks like." [Source: use-cases/uc-001-build-brain.md]

### VP5: Five-Discipline Coverage
**Statement:** The Brain serves the whole company, not just marketing. Each discipline — Marketing, Sales, PMM, Product, CS — gets its own agent guideline file with specific output formats, behavioral rules, and use patterns.

**Approved language:**
- "Five disciplines. One source of truth."
- "Marketing, Sales, PMM, Product, CS — each gets their own agent guideline."
- "Alignment means everyone, not just the marketing team."

**Forbidden language:**
- "End-to-end" (overused, vague)
- "Full-stack" (engineering term, wrong context)
- "Comprehensive solution" (buzzword soup)

**Dogfooding hook:** "Our Brain has five guideline files — one per discipline. The marketing agent produces blog posts. The sales agent produces discovery prep. The CS agent produces onboarding guides. Same source, different output." [Source: guidelines/]

**Statement:** The Brain captures the founder's knowledge in a format that persists without them. New hires read the Brain. Agents read the Brain. The founder stops being the bottleneck.

**Approved language:**

**Forbidden language:**

- "Scale the founder" (the founder is a person, not a feature)

**Dogfooding hook:** "Page Sands built Bravenger's Brain. Now agents produce aligned content without Page reviewing every piece. That's the point." [Source: personas/persona-founder.md#pains]

### VP7: Honest by Design
**Statement:** The Brain documents gaps, limitations, and partial support. Use case status fields reflect product reality, not marketing aspiration. This honesty builds trust with technical evaluators and earns credibility with customers.

**Approved language:**
- "Status reflects reality. If it's partially supported, the Brain says so."
- "Gaps are documented. That's a feature, not a bug."
- "The Brain admits what it can't do. That's how you know you can trust what it says it can."

**Forbidden language:**
- "Transparent" (overused to the point of meaninglessness)
- "No surprises" (sets an unrealistic expectation)
- "Full visibility" (sounds like a surveillance tool)

**Dogfooding hook:** "Look at UC-003 in our Brain. Status: partially-supported. Gaps: no automated content ingestion, no drift scoring algorithm. We document what we can't do because that's what makes the rest credible." [Source: use-cases/uc-003-detect-drift.md#gaps]

## Objection Handling

### Objection 1: "We already did a positioning exercise."
**Response:** A positioning exercise produces a document. Bravenger produces a system. The exercise probably generated a slide deck that lives in Google Drive. Nobody reads it. AI tools can't consume it. The Brain is a structured, agent-readable, version-controlled system that gets used daily — not a deliverable that decays.

**Proof point:** Ask them: "Can your AI tools read your positioning doc and produce aligned content?" If the answer is no, the exercise didn't produce a system. [Source: icp.md#pains]

### Objection 2: "We can build this ourselves."
**Response:** You can build the structure. Markdown files in a folder — your engineers could scaffold that in an afternoon. The architecture is open. We don't hide it.

What you can't build is the right content. A technical founder will write personas that describe their product, not their buyer. They'll write use cases that list features, not customer outcomes. They'll skip the gotchas because they haven't delivered to 10 customers yet. They'll write a messaging framework that sounds right to them but doesn't match how any of their four buyer types actually evaluate.

The Brain looks simple. The knowledge inside it is not. That's why it takes a week with someone who's done it, and a quarter with someone figuring it out for the first time.

**Proof point:** Show them this Brain. Ask two questions: "Could your team build this folder structure?" (Yes.) "Could your team write content this specific and this grounded in a week?" (No.) The structure is free. The expertise to populate it is the product. [Source: use-cases/uc-001-build-brain.md#outcome]

### Objection 3: "This is just a fancy wiki."
**Response:** A wiki is unstructured. There's no schema, no governance, no agent readability. You can put anything in a wiki — and people do. That's why wikis become graveyards. The Brain has YAML frontmatter on every file, a mandatory read order for agents, citation requirements, and discipline-specific output formats. Structure is what makes it a system instead of a wiki.

**Proof point:** Open any Brain file. Show the YAML frontmatter. Show AGENTS.md. That structure doesn't exist in a wiki.

### Objection 4: "$10-15K is expensive for a week of work."
**Response:** Compare it to the alternatives. A positioning consultant charges $15-30K for 4-8 weeks and delivers a slide deck. An agency retainer is $10-20K/month and produces content without a system. A freelance content strategist is $10K/month. Bravenger delivers a permanent asset in one week for a one-time cost. The Brain doesn't require a retainer. It persists.

**Proof point:** "How much did your last positioning exercise cost? How much of it is still in use?" [Source: icp.md#buying-process]

### Objection 5: "We don't use AI for content yet."
**Response:** You will. Every company in your market is adopting AI for content creation. The question isn't if, it's when. And when you do, the AI will need context. Without a Brain, your AI tools will produce generic, misaligned content. With a Brain, they produce accurate, cited, on-brand output from day one. Build the foundation now, and you're ready when you adopt AI.

**Proof point:** "Are your engineers using AI? Yes? Your marketing team will too. The Brain ensures they have the same quality of context."

### Objection 6: "How do we know it won't go stale like everything else?"
**Response:** Three reasons it persists: (1) It's designed to be consumed by agents, so it's part of the daily workflow — not a reference doc nobody opens. (2) The PMM or marketing lead owns it and updates it as part of their regular work. (3) Drift detection (UC-003) measures alignment, so you know when something's stale instead of discovering it by accident.

**Proof point:** "Our own Brain is our operating system. If it goes stale, our content is wrong, and we notice immediately. That's the dogfooding guarantee." [Source: use-cases/uc-003-detect-drift.md]

## Funnel-Stage Messaging

### Awareness
**Goal:** Establish the problem and the category.

- "Your positioning is trapped in your founder's head. Your team can't access it."
- "Your AI tools produce content with no context. That's not an AI problem — it's a knowledge problem."
- "Five teams. Five versions of your story. That's messaging drift."

### Consideration
**Goal:** Position the Company Brain as the right category of solution.

- "You don't need another positioning exercise. You need a system."
- "Bravenger turns your positioning doc into a system that produces aligned content for every team."
- "One week. Fixed price. A working Company Brain."

### Decision
**Goal:** Drive confidence in the specific engagement.

- "Browse our Brain. This is what we build for you. Same architecture, tailored to your company."
- "$10-15K. One week. A permanent asset your team uses every day."
- "We've made the architectural decisions so you don't have to."

### Onboarding (Post-Purchase)
**Goal:** Ensure adoption and long-term value.

- "Your Brain is live. Let's walk through the three use cases your team will use most."
- "The PMM owns the Brain going forward. Here's how to keep it current."
- "When something changes — new feature, new competitor, new persona — update one file. Everything downstream adjusts."
