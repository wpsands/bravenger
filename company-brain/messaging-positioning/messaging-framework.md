# Messaging Framework

## Value Propositions

### VP1: Scale Meets Depth — "A Focus Group with 10,000 People"
**Statement:** Natter combines the depth of qualitative research with the scale of a survey. A 7-minute peer-to-peer conversation yields 1,000+ words versus approximately 10 from a typical survey answer. That is 100x more insight per participant — at a scale of 20,000+ simultaneous conversations.

**Approved language:**
- "Not another survey. A conversation intelligence engine."
- "A 7-minute conversation yields 1,000+ words. A survey answer yields 10. The math is clear."
- "The depth of a focus group. The scale of a survey. In under an hour."
- "What people actually say to each other — not what they click on a form."

**Forbidden language:**
- "Survey replacement" (we complement, not replace — this removes defensive reactions)
- "Better survey" (positions us in the wrong category)
- "AI-powered listening" (vague, every competitor claims this)

**Persona relevance:** Strong with P-001 CHRO (board-ready insight), Strong with P-002 People Analytics (methodological breakthrough), Strong with P-003 VP Strategy (strategic intelligence), Moderate with P-004 Revenue Leader (applies differently to sales context).

### VP2: 100x More Insight Than a Survey
**Statement:** A 7-minute Natter conversation produces 1,000+ words of rich qualitative data — real opinions, real concerns, real ideas expressed in natural conversation. A typical survey open-ended response produces approximately 10 words. Per participant, Natter generates 100x more insight. Multiply by 20,000 participants and you have a qualitative data set larger than a decade of focus groups.

**Approved language:**
- "1,000+ words per conversation. 10 words per survey answer. That's not a marginal improvement."
- "More qualitative data in one session than a year of focus groups."
- "29,810 qualitative statements captured in a single study." [Source: research/employee-voice-report.md]

**Forbidden language:**
- "Big data" (wrong framing — this is rich data, not just large volume)
- "Unstructured data" (technically accurate but sounds messy to non-technical buyers)
- "Deep dive" (overused — see brand-vision.md#words-we-avoid)

**Persona relevance:** Strong with P-002 People Analytics (data quality is their obsession), Strong with P-001 CHRO (justifies investment vs. survey), Moderate with P-003 VP Strategy (cares about insight quality, not methodology).

**Statement:** Natter's AI delivers action-ready outputs — clear themes, sentiment analysis, priorities, and recommended actions — within an hour of session close. The typical survey-to-action cycle takes 3-6 months. Natter compresses this to hours, enabling leadership to act while the insight is still relevant.

**Approved language:**
- "Decision-ready insights in under an hour."
- "Action-ready outputs straight from your people's voices."
- "Your board gets insight this week, not next quarter."
- "The insight-to-action gap: months for surveys. Hours for Natter."

**Forbidden language:**
- "Real-time analytics" (implies live dashboard during conversations — the insight report comes after)
- "Instant insights" (oversimplifies the AI analysis process)
- "Automated recommendations" (sounds like it removes human judgment)

**Persona relevance:** Strong with P-001 CHRO (board timing pressure), Strong with P-003 VP Strategy (transformation decisions can't wait), Strong with P-002 People Analytics (eliminates weeks of manual coding), Moderate with P-004 Revenue Leader (sales cycles are faster but same principle).

Conversations are peer-to-peer, eliminating manager-employee power dynamics. True anonymity — not just confidentiality — means employees speak freely because the system is engineered for it.

**Approved language:**
- "True anonymity, not just confidentiality. There's a difference."
- "PII redacted at the point of transcription. Not after review. At transcription."
- "ISO 27001 certified. GDPR compliant. EU AI Act compliant."
- "Employees speak freely because the system is engineered for it."

**Forbidden language:**
- "Safe space" (overused, politically charged in some contexts — use "psychologically safe environment")
- "We guarantee anonymity" (legal teams will object to "guarantee" — use "designed for" or "engineered for")
- "Trust us" (the whole point is that the system design creates trust — you shouldn't need to ask)

**Persona relevance:** Strong with P-001 CHRO (their reputation is on the line), Strong with P-002 People Analytics (data validity depends on honest responses), Moderate with P-003 VP Strategy (cares about candid answers, less about the mechanism), Low with P-004 Revenue Leader (different context).

**Persona relevance:** Strong with P-001 CHRO (trust is the existential challenge), Strong with P-002 People Analytics (closing the loop is their hardest problem), Moderate with P-003 VP Strategy (wants transformation impact), Low with P-004 Revenue Leader (different context).

## Objection Handling

### Objection 1: "We already have Qualtrics/Glint/Peakon."
**Response:** We know. Most of our customers do too. Natter doesn't replace your survey platform — it adds the qualitative depth your surveys are missing. Your survey tells you engagement is down 4 points. Natter tells you why — in employees' own words — and gives you specific themes to act on. Think of it as the qualitative layer that makes your survey investment more valuable. Survey fatigue is not about frequency — it's about lack of tangible outcomes from feedback. [Source: research/employee-voice-report.md]

**Proof point:** "What's your response rate on open-ended survey questions? And how many words does the average response contain? If the answer is under 50%, you have a voice gap that surveys can't close. Fewer than half of leaders rate their current listening initiatives as effective." [Source: research/employee-voice-report.md] [Source: icp.md#pains]

### Objection 2: "How is this different from a survey?"
**Response:** A survey asks a question and records a click or a short text response. Natter matches two employees into a video conversation and captures what they actually say to each other — 1,000+ words in 7 minutes. AI analyzes every conversation simultaneously and delivers themes, sentiment, and priorities in under an hour. It's the difference between counting opinions and understanding them.

**Proof point:** "In one Natter study, 420 peer conversations captured 29,810 qualitative statements — 492,184 words transcribed, 96 themes generated. Compare that to 420 survey responses averaging 10 words each: 4,200 words, maybe 3 themes. Same people, 100x the insight." [Source: research/employee-voice-report.md]

### Objection 3: "My employees won't talk on video."
**Response:** This is the most common concern and the least supported by data. Natter sessions consistently achieve 80%+ participation rates — significantly higher than most survey programs. The 1:1 peer format feels fundamentally different from a town hall or a recorded interview. Employees are talking to a colleague, not presenting to an audience. And because PII is redacted at transcription, there's genuine anonymity. Employees speak freely when the format is designed for it.

**Proof point:** "Participation rates across Natter's enterprise customer base consistently exceed 80%. Employees report that the peer-to-peer format felt natural and non-threatening." Share a customer example if available.

### Objection 4: "What about data privacy?"
**Response:** Lead with credentials: ISO 27001 certified. GDPR, UK GDPR, EU AI Act compliant. CCPA aligned. PII is redacted at the point of transcription — not during analysis, not after review, at the moment the audio is transcribed. Data is anonymized, not just confidential. We can brief your DPO on the specific data flow and redaction mechanisms. This matters because 56% of conversations in Natter research cited concern about ethical use of AI for data security and privacy — it's the top concern leaders have, and Natter is built to address it. [Source: research/deloitte-white-paper.md]

**Proof point:** Walk through the data flow diagram: conversation → transcription (PII redacted here) → AI analysis → insight report. No path from insight to individual identity. Offer to share the ISO 27001 certificate and the data processing agreement.

### Objection 5: "This sounds expensive."
**Response:** The comparison matters. A McKinsey listening exercise costs $600K+ and reaches 30 people. Qualtrics enterprise costs $250K+ and gives you survey data — clicks on a form. Natter costs $200–$400K and gives you real human conversations with 20,000+ employees, AI-analyzed insights in under an hour, and board-ready reports. The question is not whether it's expensive. The question is what it costs to make a $50M transformation decision without hearing what your workforce actually thinks.

**Proof point:** "Your last engagement survey cost $X and produced Y words of qualitative data. Natter produces 100x the qualitative data at comparable cost. But the real comparison is: what's the cost of the insight you're not getting?" [Source: icp.md#budget]

### Objection 6: "We tried focus groups — our employees don't open up."
**Response:** Focus groups have structural problems that prevent honest conversation: the facilitator introduces bias, dominant voices control the room, participants know they're being observed, and group sizes of 8-12 mean most people barely speak. Natter eliminates all of these. 1:1 peer matching means no dominant voices. No facilitator means no bias. PII redaction means genuine anonymity. And the conversation is between two colleagues — the most natural form of human communication.

**Proof point:** "Focus groups hear from 8-12 people. Natter hears from 20,000. Focus groups take 6 weeks to schedule. Natter runs in under an hour. Both produce qualitative data. Only one does it at scale."

- "Join 50 CHROs for a Natter roundtable — experience the platform by participating."

**Goal:** Drive confidence in the specific investment and deployment.

**Goal:** Drive participation, insight quality, and time-to-value.

- "Your first session is designed together. Conversation prompts, matching rules, and communication templates — all configured before the first employee logs in."
- "Insight report delivered within 24 hours of your first session. Review it with your Natter CSM to calibrate themes and identify action areas."
- "Session 1 results build the case for Session 2. We help you present to leadership."

## Persona-Specific Messaging Packages

### P-001 CHRO / Chief People Officer
**Lead with:** Board-ready insight that surveys can't deliver. Scale meets depth.
**Support with:** Participation rates, peer CHRO references, insight report quality.
**Close with:** Graduated free trial — experience it through a roundtable before committing.
**Avoid:** Technical methodology details, integration architecture. The CHRO cares about outcomes.

### P-002 VP/Head of People Analytics
**Lead with:** Qualitative depth at quantitative scale. Methodological rigor.
**Support with:** AI theme validation, demographic analysis, API export, integration with analytics stack.
**Close with:** Pilot session with 30 participants to validate methodology before enterprise deployment.
**Avoid:** Vague AI claims, unsupported precision, anything that feels methodologically unsound.

### P-003 VP Strategy / Chief Transformation Officer
**Lead with:** Strategic intelligence, not employee engagement. Transformation readiness data.
**Support with:** Speed (decision-ready in hours), longitudinal tracking, board-ready export.
**Close with:** Anchor against consulting costs ($600K+ for 30 interviews vs. $200K for 20,000 conversations).
**Avoid:** HR language (engagement, sentiment, employee voice). Frame as strategic intelligence.

### P-004 Head of Revenue / VP Sales
**Lead with:** Peer-to-peer sales coaching at scale. Customer voice from 500 customers, not 12.
**Support with:** Win/loss pattern analysis, rep ramp acceleration, pipeline generated from customer roundtables.
**Close with:** Revenue impact metrics — win rate, ramp time, customer intelligence.
**Avoid:** HR framing entirely. Use revenue language: pipeline, win rate, quota, coaching.

## Sales Methodology Reference

Natter's sales methodology has 4 pillars, encoded here for agent reference:

### 1. BANT (Qualify)
Budget, Authority, Need, Timeline. Pre-pipeline gate. Every opportunity must clear BANT before entering the pipeline. [Source: guidelines/sales-usage.md]

### 2. PREDICT (Manage)
7 dimensions scored R/A/G (Red/Amber/Green):
- **P**roblem — Is the pain confirmed and quantified?
- **R**elationships — Do we have champion + economic buyer access?
- **E**ngagement — Are they actively participating in the evaluation?
- **D**ecision process — Do we understand the procurement path?
- **I**nternal processes — Are legal, security, and IT aligned?
- **C**ompetitors — Do we know who else is being evaluated?
- **T**iming — Is there a compelling event driving the timeline?

Auto-updated from call transcripts + email + Salesforce. [Source: guidelines/sales-usage.md]

### 3. BATNA (Negotiate)
Best Alternative to a Negotiated Agreement. Strong PREDICT = weak customer BATNA = hold price. The graduated free trial builds PREDICT strength organically — by Session 2, the customer has already experienced the value. [Source: guidelines/sales-usage.md]

### 4. Forecast
"Will we win?" (PREDICT health) × "When?" (timing confidence). Commit / Best Case / Pipeline auto-categorized. [Source: guidelines/sales-usage.md]

### Friday Flywheel
1. Friday AM: Auto-assembly of each rep's week from Fathom + Salesforce + email
2. Friday PM: 5-min micro-journal (The Win, The Friction, The Surprise, One Priority)
3. Friday evening: Auto-generated Weekly GTM Digest
4. Monday: 30-min meeting — deal clinic, playbook tension, priorities → Brain commits

[Source: guidelines/sales-usage.md]
