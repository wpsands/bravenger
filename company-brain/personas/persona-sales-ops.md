# The Sales Ops Leader

## Summary

The Sales Ops or RevOps Manager at a 100-500 person B2B company. They are the operational backbone of the sales organization. They own the CRM, the data, the reports, and the processes that keep the revenue engine running. They are deeply frustrated with CRM data quality and spend their days cleaning up after reps who won't log activities properly.

They are the champion for NovaCRM. They evaluate tools, run the implementation, build the business case for leadership, and maintain the system post-deployment. If they don't believe in the product, the deal never reaches the VP Sales. If they do believe, they will sell it internally harder than any NovaCRM rep ever could.

## Pains

1. **"I spend 40% of my time cleaning CRM data."** Reps don't fill in required fields. They update deal stages wrong. Contact information is stale. The Sales Ops leader is constantly running cleanup scripts, chasing reps for updates, and manually correcting reports.

2. **"Our reports are only as good as what reps put in."** Leadership wants real-time dashboards. But the data underneath is garbage. The Sales Ops leader knows the numbers are wrong but can't fix the input problem. They end up manually adjusting reports, which means they own the number.

3. **"We have 12 tools and none of them talk to each other."** The sales stack is a mess: CRM, engagement platform, conversation intelligence, intent data, forecasting tool. Every integration is fragile. When one breaks, the Sales Ops leader is the one who gets paged.

4. **"Reps see the CRM as punishment, not a tool."** Every attempt to improve data quality adds friction for reps. Required fields slow them down. Mandatory logging feels like surveillance. The Sales Ops leader is caught between leadership's need for data and reps' resistance to process.

5. **"Migration is my nightmare scenario."** They've been through a CRM migration before — or they've heard the horror stories. Months of data mapping, parallel running, broken workflows, lost historical data, and reps who refuse to adopt the new system. They would rather fix the current CRM than switch.

6. **"I can't get engineering resources for integrations."** They need custom integrations, API work, or data pipelines. Engineering has a 6-month backlog and sales tools are never the priority. The Sales Ops leader ends up building brittle Zapier automations and hoping they don't break.

## Decision Process

- **Speed:** Methodical. They will run a thorough evaluation over 6-10 weeks. They test everything. They map every field. They check every integration.
- **Evaluation criteria:** Data model flexibility. API quality and documentation. Migration tooling. Integration with existing stack (Slack, email, calendar, BI tools). Admin experience. How much manual work does it eliminate vs. create?
- **Budget sensitivity:** They don't control budget, but they control the recommendation. They build the ROI model. They calculate total cost of ownership including their own implementation time.
- **Trust signals:** Detailed API documentation. A sandbox environment they can test. Migration case studies with specific data points (X records migrated, Y weeks, Z% data accuracy). A responsive technical support team during evaluation.
- **Blockers:** If the migration path is unclear or risky. If the API is underdocumented. If the admin experience is worse than their current CRM. If it adds more manual work than it eliminates.

## Messaging Dos and Don'ts

### Do
- Lead with data quality. "What if reps didn't need to log activities manually because the CRM already captured them?" This is the promise that gets their attention.
- Show the admin experience. They'll be living in this tool daily. A clean, powerful admin panel matters as much as the rep experience.
- Provide a detailed migration playbook. Step by step. Timeline. Risk mitigation. Parallel running strategy. This is what they'll present to leadership.
- Talk about integrations concretely. "We have a native Slack integration, a REST API with 200+ endpoints, and pre-built connectors for Outreach, Gong, and Snowflake." Specifics, not handwaving.
- Acknowledge their expertise. They know CRM operations better than the NovaCRM sales rep does. Treat them as a technical peer.
- Show how AI reduces their workload. Auto-populated fields, intelligent activity capture, automated data enrichment — fewer cleanup hours per week.

### Don't
- Don't skip the operational details to get to the AI pitch. They care about data model, field mapping, and workflow automation before they care about AI scoring.
- Don't minimize migration complexity. "It's easy!" is a red flag. Show that you understand how hard it is, then show how you make it manageable.
- Don't promise zero-effort adoption. They know reps resist change. Show adoption strategies, not shortcuts.
- Don't present ROI numbers without showing the methodology. They'll build their own model. Give them the inputs.
- Don't go over their head to the VP Sales before they're ready. They want to be the champion, not the person who got bypassed.
- Don't ignore their current stack. Ask what they're using and show how NovaCRM fits — or honestly say where it doesn't.

## Objections and Responses

| Objection | Response Approach |
|-----------|-------------------|
| **"The migration will take 6 months and break everything."** | Acknowledge the fear — it's based on real experience. Then walk through the NovaCRM migration toolkit: automated field mapping, data validation, parallel running. Share specific timelines from beta customers: "Company X migrated 50K deals from HubSpot in 4 weeks." |
| **"Our Salesforce instance is too customized to leave."** | Ask what percentage of those customizations are actually used. Most Salesforce instances have accumulated years of unused fields, triggers, and workflows. NovaCRM migration is a chance to clean house. Help them audit what matters vs. what's legacy. |
| **"I don't have time to evaluate another tool."** | Respect their time. Offer a low-effort evaluation: a sandbox with their data schema pre-loaded, API documentation they can review asynchronously, and a migration assessment that takes 2 hours, not 2 weeks. |
| **"What happens if NovaCRM doesn't work out?"** | Data export is available at any time in standard formats. No vendor lock-in. If they need to leave, their data leaves with them. This is a genuine differentiator against Salesforce's data portability challenges. |
| **"The AI features sound nice but I need basic CRM to work first."** | Agree completely. Lead with the operational fundamentals: data model, workflow automation, reporting, integrations. The AI is a layer on top of a solid CRM foundation, not a substitute for one. |

## Day in the Life

Understanding how this persona spends their time reveals where NovaCRM adds or removes friction.

- **8:00 AM** — Opens CRM admin panel. Checks data health alerts, integration status, and overnight sync errors. This is the first thing they see every day.
- **9:00 AM** — Builds or updates reports for the VP Sales. Pipeline coverage, forecast roll-up, rep activity compliance. Spends 30-60 minutes on what should take 5 minutes.
- **10:30 AM** — Troubleshoots broken integrations or data issues. A Zapier automation failed overnight. Lead routing rules need updating. A rep merged duplicate contacts incorrectly.
- **12:00 PM** — Joins the sales team's forecast call to provide data support. Answers "why does the dashboard show X?" questions.
- **2:00 PM** — Works on process improvements: new lead scoring rules, territory realignment, onboarding workflow for new reps.
- **4:00 PM** — Reviews tool vendor evaluations, reads community forums, or tests a new integration in the sandbox.

## Product / UI / UX Hooks

These are the features and design decisions that will resonate with this persona.

| Hook | Why It Matters |
|------|---------------|
| **Automatic activity capture** | NovaCRM logs emails, calls, and meetings without rep input. This solves the #1 pain: data quality depends on reps remembering to log. |
| **Flexible data model** | Custom objects, custom fields, and relationships that match their actual sales process — not a rigid structure they have to work around. |
| **Migration toolkit** | Field mapping wizard, data validation, parallel running mode, rollback capability. This is the feature that gets the champion to say yes. |
| **API-first architecture** | 200+ REST API endpoints with comprehensive documentation. They can build whatever integrations they need without waiting for engineering. |
| **Admin dashboard** | Real-time data health scores, adoption metrics by rep, integration status, and automated alerts when something breaks. |
| **Workflow automation builder** | Visual workflow builder for lead routing, stage progression, notifications, and escalations. Replaces their Zapier automations with native tooling. |
| **Smart field suggestions** | AI suggests field values based on email and call context. Reps confirm with one click instead of typing. Reduces friction while improving data quality. |
| **Comparison mode during migration** | Side-by-side view of old CRM vs. NovaCRM data during parallel running. Lets them validate that nothing was lost. |
