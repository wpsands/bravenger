# The Revenue Leader

## Summary

The Head of Revenue or VP Sales at a Fortune 500 or large enterprise organization. They own revenue operations, sales coaching, and customer intelligence. They are not the primary buyer for Natter's People solution — but they are the buyer for Natter's Revenue solution, which uses the same conversation intelligence platform for sales coaching, win/loss analysis, and customer voice capture.

They are a use case sponsor for Natter. In many organizations, the Revenue solution is sold as an expansion after the People solution is established. In others, it's the entry point — particularly for professional services firms where client-facing research roundtables generate both intelligence and pipeline simultaneously.

## Pains

1. **"I can't coach at scale."** The VP Sales has 200+ reps across multiple regions. They can't be in every room. Frontline managers coach inconsistently. There's no systematic way to ensure coaching quality across the organization. What works for one team stays locked in that team.

2. **"Win/loss analysis is anecdotal."** When a deal closes or a deal dies, the reasons recorded in the CRM are surface-level. "Competitor won on price." "Champion left." The real story — what happened in the conversations, what objections weren't overcome, what the customer actually cared about — is scattered across call recordings and email threads that nobody has time to analyze.

3. **"Customer voice is trapped in CRM notes."** Reps capture customer feedback in meeting notes that nobody reads. Customer advisory boards are limited to 12 people who don't represent the full customer base. The VP Sales knows the customer voice contains strategic intelligence about product gaps, competitive threats, and expansion opportunities — but has no systematic way to capture it.

4. **"Sales enablement content is generic."** Enablement creates playbooks based on assumptions about what customers care about. The VP Sales wants enablement content built from actual customer conversations — real objections, real priorities, real language. Not what marketing thinks customers say, but what they actually say.

5. **"Peer learning doesn't scale."** The best coaching happens when reps learn from each other. But structured peer learning — rep-to-rep conversations about what's working, what's not, and how to handle specific situations — is impossible to orchestrate across a large sales organization without a platform.

## Decision Process

- **Speed:** Fast. Revenue leaders are action-oriented. If the platform can demonstrate value within a quarter, they'll move.
- **Evaluation criteria:** Can it run structured peer-to-peer coaching conversations across 200+ reps? Can it summarize win/loss patterns from customer conversations? Can it host customer roundtables that generate intelligence? Is the ROI measurable in revenue terms?
- **Budget sensitivity:** Revenue leaders have enablement and coaching budgets. $100K–$200K for a coaching and intelligence platform is justifiable if it can show impact on win rate or rep ramp time.
- **Trust signals:** Named customers using Natter for revenue solutions. Measurable outcomes (win rate improvement, ramp time reduction, customer intelligence generated). Integration with existing sales stack (Salesforce, Gong, Outreach).
- **Blockers:** If the CHRO owns Natter and doesn't want Sales using it independently. If the platform can't integrate with the sales tech stack. If the output is HR-formatted instead of sales-formatted.

- Proof of scale — not a 10-person pilot

## Messaging Dos and Don'ts

### Do
- Lead with revenue impact. "What if every win/loss pattern across 200 reps was captured, analyzed, and turned into coaching content — automatically?"
- Show the sales coaching use case. Structured peer-to-peer coaching conversations where reps share best practices, coached by AI-generated talking points based on their deal data.
- Demonstrate the customer roundtable motion. 50 customers matched into peer conversations about their experience, generating intelligence that informs product roadmap and surfaces expansion opportunities.
- Use revenue language. Pipeline, win rate, quota attainment, ramp time, deal velocity. Not engagement or sentiment.
- Anchor against current coaching costs. "You're flying 8 managers to an offsite once a year. Natter runs 200 coaching conversations in an afternoon."

### Don't
- Don't lead with the People/HR story. If this persona thinks Natter is "an HR tool," they'll deprioritize it.
- Don't use HR language. No "engagement," "sentiment," or "employee voice" when talking to this persona. Use "customer voice," "rep coaching," "win/loss intelligence."
- Don't oversell AI. Show what the AI does (summarize conversations, identify patterns, generate coaching prompts) and let the results speak.
- Don't propose a 6-month rollout. Revenue leaders want impact this quarter.
- Don't ignore the existing sales tech stack. Ask what they use (Gong, Salesforce, Outreach) and show integration.

## Objections and Responses

| Objection | Response Approach |
|-----------|-------------------|
| **"We already have Gong for this."** | "Gong records what reps say to customers. Natter runs structured conversations between reps — peer-to-peer coaching at scale — and between customers — voice of customer roundtables. They're complementary. Gong captures the call. Natter creates the conversation." |
| **"Coaching is my managers' job."** | "Absolutely. Natter makes your managers more effective by giving them AI-generated coaching insights from peer conversations. Instead of one manager coaching 15 reps based on the 3 calls they observed, every rep gets coached based on patterns from 200 peer conversations." |
| **"Customer advisory boards already give us voice of customer."** | "CABs have 12 customers who don't represent the full base. Natter runs 500 customers through peer conversations in an afternoon and surfaces patterns you'd never see from 12 people. Plus, the customers love it — they learn from each other while you capture intelligence." |
| **"How do I measure ROI?"** | "Three metrics: (1) Win rate change after reps participate in Natter coaching conversations. (2) Ramp time reduction for new reps exposed to peer best practices. (3) Pipeline generated from customer roundtables where participants become champions for expansion." |

## Day in the Life

- **7:00 AM** — Checks pipeline dashboard, overnight deal movement, and forecast risk alerts.
- **8:30 AM** — Revenue leadership standup. Reviews at-risk deals, this week's commit, and coaching priorities.
- **10:00 AM** — Manager one-on-ones. Reviews deal performance, coaching coverage, and rep development plans.
- **12:00 PM** — Customer meetings or field rides. Directly involved in strategic accounts.
- **2:00 PM** — Forecast call with CFO or CEO. Needs accurate, data-backed numbers.
- **4:00 PM** — Reviews win/loss data, sales enablement content, and competitive intelligence.
- **5:30 PM** — Reads LinkedIn, reviews vendor outreach, responds to peer messages.

## Product / UI / UX Hooks

| Hook | Why It Matters |
|------|---------------|
| **Peer-to-peer sales coaching sessions** | Structured conversations where reps share best practices, coached by AI-generated prompts based on deal data and win patterns. This is the core product feature for this persona. |
| **Win/loss AI analysis** | AI summarizes patterns from customer conversations post-deal: what drove the win, what caused the loss, what competitors said. Delivered as actionable coaching content, not raw transcripts. |
| **Customer roundtable platform** | Host 50-500 customers in peer-to-peer conversations. Generates NPS-quality intelligence at scale while giving customers a valuable peer experience. Dual value: intelligence + relationship building. |
| **Sales-formatted dashboards** | Output formatted for revenue reviews: win rate trends, coaching impact by rep, customer sentiment by segment, competitive mention frequency. Not HR dashboards. |
| **Salesforce and Gong integration** | Natter data flows into Salesforce (customer intelligence on the account record) and enriches Gong (peer coaching insights alongside call recordings). |
| **Rep ramp acceleration** | New reps participate in Natter coaching conversations with top performers within their first week. AI generates personalized coaching recommendations based on the conversation. |
