# The Head of Marketing

## Summary

The Head of Marketing is the first marketing leader at a Series A/B B2B SaaS company. They were hired to "build the marketing function." In practice, this means they own brand, content, demand gen, website, events, and often product marketing — with a team of 1-4 people. They are resource-constrained, expected to ship immediately, and working without a foundation.

They are the champion and often the day-to-day buyer for Bravenger. They feel the pain of messaging drift most acutely because they're the one producing content and watching it contradict what sales says.

## Pains

1. **"I inherited nothing."** There's a blog with random posts, a pitch deck from 2 years ago, and a Google Doc someone titled "messaging v3 FINAL final." Nothing is usable. Nothing is current. They're starting from below zero.

2. **"I own everything and I'm drowning."** Brand, content, demand gen, website, events, product marketing, analyst relations — all one person or a tiny team. They can't do any of it well because they're doing all of it.

3. **"The founder has the positioning in their head and won't write it down."** They've asked. Multiple times. The founder says "yeah, I'll get to it." They haven't. So the marketer guesses, gets it wrong, and the founder corrects them verbally, which doesn't scale.

4. **"AI tools make my team faster but less accurate."** They started using AI for blog posts and social. The output rate tripled. But the messaging is inconsistent, the value props drift, and now they're publishing more mistakes faster.

5. **"Sales doesn't use what I build."** They created battle cards, one-pagers, and email templates. Sales ignored all of it and wrote their own. Now there are two versions of every message, and they don't match.

6. **"I can't prove that messaging is working or broken."** There's no measurement for alignment. They suspect content is off-brand but can't quantify it. When they raise it, people shrug.

## Decision Process

- **Speed:** Moderate. They want to move fast but need to bring the founder along for budget approval.
- **Evaluation criteria:** Will this make my life easier? Can my team use it without a PhD? Will the founder actually buy in? Does it produce usable output on day one?
- **Budget sensitivity:** $10-15K is meaningful but not prohibitive. They'll compare it to an agency retainer or a freelance content strategist. If it produces a permanent asset, the math works.
- **Trust signals:** Case studies from similar companies, clear deliverables, realistic timeline, evidence that the founder will engage.
- **Blockers:** If it requires heavy time investment from their team during the build week. If it feels like another positioning exercise that produces a deck nobody reads.

## Messaging Dos and Don'ts

### Do
- Lead with empathy for their situation: "You were hired to build marketing from scratch. That's hard. We give you the foundation."
- Show tangible output: "Here's what a Brain produces — blog posts, email campaigns, sales enablement — all from one source."
- Emphasize time savings: "One week. You get a system that would take 3 months to build yourself."
- Use their language: brand, content engine, demand gen, pipeline, campaign, enablement.
- Position as a force multiplier for their small team, not a replacement.

### Don't
- Don't lead with technical architecture (repos, YAML, Markdown). Translate it into outcomes.
- Don't assume they understand agent architecture. Explain what "agents read the Brain" means in practice.
- Don't dismiss their existing efforts. They've been doing their best with nothing. Validate that.
- Don't make them feel like they should have built this themselves. The founder should have written it down. They're not the problem.
- Don't use engineering jargon. "Schema" means nothing to them. "Structure" means everything.

## Product / UI / UX Hooks

These are the features and design decisions that will resonate with this persona.

| Hook | Why It Matters |
|------|---------------|
| **Clear file organization** | They need to find things fast. A well-structured repo with a README file map is intuitive even for non-technical users. |
| **Examples directory** | Approved messaging examples they can copy, adapt, and use immediately. This is what they'll open first. |
| **Agent output that cites sources** | When the agent produces a blog post and cites `[Source: personas/persona-founder.md#pains]`, they can trust it and verify it. |
| **Governance rules** | AGENTS.md gives them control over what agents can and can't say. This is brand governance they've never had before. |
| **Messaging framework** | Value props, approved language, objection handling — all in one place. This is what they've been trying to build. |
| **Visual-friendly format** | Markdown renders nicely in GitHub, Notion imports, and most tools they already use. They don't need CLI comfort. |
| **One-week delivery** | They need to show progress to the founder fast. A week is perfect. |
| **Built-in drift detection** | The concept of measuring alignment gives them a way to prove their work matters. |
