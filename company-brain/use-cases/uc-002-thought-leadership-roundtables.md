# UC-002: Thought Leadership & Executive Roundtables

## Trigger

This use case is triggered by two distinct needs — one external (Natter's own GTM motion) and one internal (customer-hosted roundtables):

**As Natter's GTM engine (graduated free trial):**
- Natter marketing identifies a strategic topic relevant to the target persona (e.g., "GenAI and the Workforce," "The Future of Employee Listening," "Transformation Readiness in 2026").
- Natter invites 30-200 senior executives from target accounts to participate in a peer-to-peer roundtable.
- The roundtable IS the product demo. Executives experience Natter by participating. The insight report demonstrates the platform's value.
- The graduated free trial path: Roundtable (experience the product) → Session 1 (30 participants inside their org) → Session 2 (20,000 participants) → MSA conversation.

**As a customer use case:**
- An organization wants to host executive roundtables on strategic topics — internal leadership alignment sessions, industry research, stakeholder engagement, or policy consultation.
- A professional services firm wants to host client-facing roundtables that generate intelligence and strengthen relationships simultaneously.
- An industry body or association wants to run a large-scale consultation with members.

## Current State

Before Natter, executive roundtables look like this:

- **Limited to 20-30 people.** A consulting firm or event organizer hosts a dinner, a half-day workshop, or a virtual panel. 20-30 executives attend. A moderator guides the conversation. A few dominant voices drive the discussion. A consultant takes notes. A report is written 6-8 weeks later.
- **No peer-to-peer structure.** Traditional roundtables are panel discussions or facilitated group conversations. Executives listen to a keynote, then discuss in groups of 8-10. The format favors the loudest voices and the most senior people. Quieter participants — who often have the most nuanced perspectives — stay silent.
- **Insight extraction is manual and slow.** A team of consultants reviews notes, synthesizes themes, and writes a report. This takes 4-8 weeks. By the time the report is published, the insights are stale.
- **Cost is prohibitive for scale.** McKinsey charges $600K+ for a listening exercise that reaches 30 people. Deloitte's rates are similar. The cost per insight is astronomical.
- **No repeatable methodology.** Each roundtable is bespoke. There's no platform that allows organizations to run executive roundtables repeatedly, at scale, with consistent methodology and AI-powered analysis.
- **The experience doesn't sell anything.** Traditional roundtables are networking events. Natter roundtables are product demonstrations disguised as peer experiences. The insight report that participants receive IS the sales proof point.

## Outcome

After deploying Natter for executive roundtables:

- **30-200 executives participate simultaneously.** Each is matched into 1:1 peer conversations with other executives on the topic. A CHRO from a fintech company talks with a CHRO from a healthcare company. The cross-pollination produces insight that siloed conversations miss.
- **AI-generated insight report within 48 hours.** Themes, sentiment, consensus areas, and divergence points — all extracted from the actual words executives said to each other. Not a consultant's interpretation. The executives' own voices.
- **The experience IS the product demo.** Executives who participate in a Natter roundtable understand immediately what the platform does. They felt the quality of the conversation. They see the quality of the insight report. The sales conversation shifts from "let me show you a demo" to "you already experienced it — shall we run this inside your organization?"
- **Graduated trial converts to MSA.** Roundtable attendee → Session 1 with 30 of their employees → Session 2 with 20,000 employees → MSA. Each step builds confidence and demonstrates value on their own data.
- **Thought leadership authority.** Natter-hosted roundtables produce publishable research: "What 142 Senior Workforce Leaders Think About AI and Employees" [Source: research/deloitte-white-paper.md]. This content feeds marketing, builds brand authority, and attracts new roundtable participants.
- **Customer-hosted roundtables generate intelligence.** Enterprise customers use the platform to host their own roundtables — internal leadership sessions, customer advisory events, stakeholder consultations — using the same AI-powered methodology.

### Interview vs. Conversation — Why Natter's Format Wins
Research comparing traditional interviews with Natter peer conversations revealed five key differences that validate the platform thesis: [Source: research/employee-voice-report.md]

1. **Breadth vs. Depth:** Interviews captured broad themes but lacked nuanced exploration. Conversations went deeper into cultural barriers, psychological safety, and actionable strategies.
2. **Problem vs. Solution focus:** Interviews centered on identifying problems. Conversations emphasized solutions — practical ways forward, multi-channel feedback systems, inclusive designs.
3. **Cultural/Geographic perspectives:** Conversations uniquely highlighted cultural and geographic distinctions. This was absent in interviews.
4. **Employee-centric vs. Organizational-centric:** Conversations explored employee wellbeing, mental health, job satisfaction. Interviews leaned toward organizational/system considerations.
5. **Obligation vs. Motivation:** Interviewees are reactive respondents to an authority. Conversation participants are proactive collaborators motivated to teach and learn from peers.

## Gotchas

- **Participant quality determines insight quality.** A roundtable with 50 CHROs from Fortune 500 companies produces fundamentally different insight than one with 50 mid-level HR managers. Invitation curation is critical.
- **Topic design matters.** "What keeps you up at night?" produces generic responses. "What is the single biggest barrier to GenAI adoption in your workforce?" produces actionable insight. Session design must be specific and provocative.
- **Follow-up is the conversion mechanism.** The roundtable is a top-of-funnel event. Without structured follow-up — sharing the insight report, offering a Session 1, connecting the prospect with a Natter rep — the conversion opportunity is lost. The roundtable alone is not enough.
- **Brand association is essential.** Natter-hosted roundtables must be clearly branded as Natter events. If the branding is too subtle, participants don't connect the experience to the platform. If it's too aggressive, participants feel sold to. Balance is critical.
- **Scheduling senior executives is hard.** CHROs and VPs Strategy are heavily scheduled. Roundtable invitations must lead with the peer value ("Join 50 CHROs for a conversation about..."), not the vendor value ("Natter invites you to experience our platform").

## Gaps

- **Hybrid in-person + virtual roundtables.** Currently, Natter roundtables are virtual. Some executives prefer in-person events. A hybrid mode — some participants in-person, some virtual — is on the roadmap.
- **Multi-round conversation formats.** Current roundtables are a single round of 1:1 conversations. A format with multiple rounds (different pairings each round) would produce richer, more diverse insight. On the roadmap.
- **White-label roundtable hosting.** Enterprise customers who want to host their own roundtables under their brand need white-label capabilities. Currently requires Natter branding.
- **Automated roundtable-to-pipeline tracking.** The conversion from roundtable attendee to Session 1 to MSA is tracked manually in Salesforce. Automated tracking and analytics for the graduated trial funnel is on the roadmap.
